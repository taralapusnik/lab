20  Digestive System

Figure 20.1 The Digestive System.

20.1 Objectives

In these laboratory exercises, you will be studying the structure of the organs of the digestive system via models, diagrams, and histology images.

By the end of this lab, you should:

  • Describe the overall function of the digestive system.
  • Describe the functions of the individual organs and structures of the digestive system.
  • Identify the individual organs of the alimentary canal on a model, in a picture or diagram or in a specimen, and describe the function of each.
  • Identify (on a model, in a picture or diagram, or in a microscope slide) and describe the histological structure of the alimentary canal, and the specific components (layers) that comprise it.
    • Mucosa
    • Submucosa
    • Muscularis externa
    • Serosa or adventitia
  • Identify the accessory organs of digestion on a model, in a picture or diagram, or in a specimen, and describe the function of each.
  • List and identify the histological specializations of the stomach and the small intestine and describe how these specializations contribute to the function of each organ.
  • Identify the histological structure of the stomach, small intestine, pancreas, and liver.
  • List select digestive enzymes involved in the digestion of proteins, fats, and carbohydrates, and identify their site of origin.

    2. small intestine
    3. ascending colon

    3. transverse colon
    4. descending colon
  6. Which of these is most associated with villi?
    1. haustra
    2. lacteals
    3. bacterial flora
    4. intestinal glands

    1. mouth
    2. esophagus
    3. stomach
    4. small intestine

    1. small intestine
    2. gallbladder
    3. liver
